How to get started

SEAD contains a vast amount of data, you sort through and view parts of this data by applying filters to it. As you apply your filters a data visualization in either tabular or cartographic format will be displayed on the right. If you have not applied any filters, nothing will be shown, since showing all the data in the database would not be beneficial.

Start by selecting at least one filter through the filter menu in the top left corner. You then need to make a selection in this filter in order to activate the rendering of a result view. For a list based filter, simply click on the rows you like to apply as filters. For a diagram/span filter, select the span you would like to view by sliding the two handles at the bottom of the filter. For a cartographic filter, create a polygon selecting an area of interesting by holding the CTRL/CMD-key and left clicking.

You may also find it beneficial to activate the tutorial mode, which you will find in the settings menu. This will show tooltips at certain points to help explain things.

The Strategic Environmental Archeology Database

SEAD is a national research infrastructure for archaeology and an international standard database for environmental archaeology data. It is being developed and managed at the Environmental Archaeology Lab (MAL), in collaboration with Humlab at Umeå University. Development and data ingestion is progressing in partnership with The Laboratory for Ceramic Research and National laboratory for wood anatomy and dendrochronology at Lund University, Sweden. Future development plans include integration with the Swedish National Heritage Board's information systems and the capacity to serve as a data source for any system.

The system allows the online storage, extraction, analysis and visualisation of data on past climates, environments and human impacts, and forms part of an international network of research infrastructure for environmental archaeology and Quaternary palaeoecology. Our mission is to provide online tools to aid international researchers in these tasks, and to provide access to data that are currently not accessible online. Please click the buttons above for more information.

SEAD is the official database infrastructure of the ArchLab Consortium of Archaeological Research Laboratories, a partner in the Neotoma Paleoecology Database and Community and the newly established NSF funded network for Building Cyberinfrastructure for Transdisciplinary Research and Visualization of the Long-Term Human Ecodynamics of the North Atlantic.

SEAD has been financed by The Swedish Research Council, Umeå University Faculty of Arts and the Department of Historical, Philosophical and Religious Studies. Component projects have been funded by Lund University.

SEAD is always interested in hearing from anyone, including students and interns, wishing to undertake projects involving the database. See people for more information. Please contact the team should you wish to make your data available through the system, or work with us in any other capacity.
